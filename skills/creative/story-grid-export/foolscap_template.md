# The Foolscap Global Story Grid for Screenwriting

The Foolscap Method is a one-page outline that captures your entire story. If you can't fit it on one page, you don't understand your story well enough yet.

## How to Fill Out the Foolscap

### Step 1: Define Your Genres

Choose from the Five-Leaf Clover:
- **External Genre**: Action, Horror, Crime, Thriller, Western, War, Society, Love, Performance
- **Internal Genre**: Worldview (Education/Maturation/Revelation/Disillusionment), Morality (Punitive/Redemption/Testing), Status (Pathetic/Sentimental/Tragic/Admiration)

### Step 2: Define Your Values

Map out the full spectrum for each genre:

**External Value Example (Thriller)**:
Life → Unconsciousness → Death → Damnation

**Internal Value Example (Disillusionment)**:
Belief → Doubt → Disillusionment → Despair

### Step 3: Identify Objects of Desire

**Conscious Want (External)**: What the protagonist actively pursues
- Save the victim
- Win the case
- Get the girl/guy
- Win the championship

**Subconscious Need (Internal)**: What the protagonist truly needs (often unknown to them)
- Redemption for past failure
- Self-acceptance
- Learn to trust
- Prove self-worth

### Step 4: Craft Your Controlling Idea

One sentence that captures theme. Format:
"[Value] is [achieved/lost] when [specific cause]."

Examples:
- "Justice prevails when an ordinary person is more clever than the system." (The Firm)
- "Love conquers all when we surrender our fear of vulnerability." (When Harry Met Sally)
- "Power corrupts absolutely when ambition overrides family loyalty." (The Godfather)
- "Narcissistic self-abuse annihilates all forms of human love." (The Shining)

### Step 5: List Conventions and Obligatory Scenes

Reference `genre_guide.md` for genre-specific requirements.

### Step 6: Map Beginning Hook, Middle Build, Ending Payoff

Fill in the Five Commandments for each section.

## Example Foolscap: THE SILENCE OF THE LAMBS

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                        FOOLSCAP GLOBAL STORY GRID
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TITLE: The Silence of the Lambs

EXTERNAL GENRE: Thriller          SUBGENRE: Serial Killer
EXTERNAL VALUE: Life → Unconsciousness → Death → Damnation

INTERNAL GENRE: Worldview         SUBGENRE: Disillusionment
INTERNAL VALUE: Belief → Doubt → Disillusionment → Despair

POINT OF VIEW: Clarice Starling (primary), with shifts to Crawford, Lecter, Gumb

PROTAGONIST'S OBJECTS OF DESIRE:
  Conscious (Want): Save Catherine Martin / Catch Buffalo Bill / Prove herself to FBI
  Subconscious (Need): Silence the screaming lambs (overcome childhood trauma)

CONTROLLING IDEA/THEME: 
We can save the innocent only by striking a bargain with the devil—but the cost
is our naïve belief in the institutions we serve.

CONVENTIONS:                          OBLIGATORY SCENES:
1. MacGuffin (Buffalo Bill's suit)    1. Inciting crime (victim discovered)
2. Red herrings                       2. Hero investigates
3. Clock (Catherine's deadline)       3. Speech in praise of villain (Lecter)
4. Speech in praise of villain        4. Hero becomes victim (personal threat)
5. Hero becomes victim                5. Hero at mercy of villain (basement)
6. False ending(s)                    6. False ending x2

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
BEGINNING HOOK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INCITING INCIDENT (Causal):
Jack Crawford assigns trainee Clarice Starling to interview Hannibal Lecter.

PROGRESSIVE COMPLICATIONS:
Lecter toys with Clarice, refusing to help. Chilton undermines her. Lecter 
gives cryptic clue ("Look deep within yourself"). Another victim found.

TURNING POINT (Revelation):
Clarice discovers the head in the storage unit—Klaus's former patient.

CRISIS (Best Bad Choice):
Return to Lecter (risking psychological manipulation) or pursue case without him?

CLIMAX:
Clarice returns to Lecter, agrees to his quid pro quo.

RESOLUTION:
Lecter provides information. The game is on.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
MIDDLE BUILD
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INCITING INCIDENT:
Catherine Martin (Senator's daughter) is kidnapped—clock starts.

PROGRESSIVE COMPLICATIONS:
Political pressure mounts. Lecter manipulates everyone. Clarice gets closer to 
truth while revealing her own trauma. Chilton makes deal with Lecter.

TURNING POINT (Revelation):
Lecter reveals "Billy" wants to become a woman—points to transformation theme.

CRISIS (Irreconcilable Goods):
Follow FBI protocol and chain of command OR trust Lecter's clues independently?

CLIMAX (All Is Lost):
Lecter escapes. FBI has wrong location. Clarice is removed from case.

RESOLUTION:
Clarice alone with her notes—all official channels closed.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ENDING PAYOFF
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INCITING INCIDENT:
Clarice follows her own lead to "Mrs. Lippman's" house—discovers Jame Gumb.

PROGRESSIVE COMPLICATIONS:
Realizes she's at Gumb's house alone. Catherine in the pit. Lights go out.

TURNING POINT (Action):
Clarice fires blindly toward Gumb's breathing in the darkness.

CRISIS (Best Bad Choice):
Fire and possibly hit Catherine OR wait and be killed?

CLIMAX:
Clarice kills Jame Gumb, rescues Catherine.

RESOLUTION:
Clarice graduates. Receives call from Lecter (he's free). The lambs are 
silent—but at what cost? She's now forever connected to Lecter.
```

## Foolscap Quick Reference

### Beginning Hook Must:
- Introduce protagonist and their ordinary world
- Present the Inciting Incident that disrupts balance
- Establish stakes and genre conventions
- End with protagonist committed to the quest (Point of No Return #1)

### Middle Build Must:
- Escalate complications progressively
- Test the protagonist's commitment
- Introduce or develop subplots
- Culminate in the "All Is Lost" moment (Point of No Return #2)
- This is where most scripts fail—complications don't escalate enough

### Ending Payoff Must:
- Resolve the global crisis
- Deliver the obligatory core event of the genre
- Show protagonist's transformation (or lack thereof)
- Provide catharsis through surprising yet inevitable climax

## Common Foolscap Mistakes

1. **Vague Objects of Desire**: "Wants to be happy" → Too abstract. Make it concrete.
2. **Controlling Idea Too Complex**: If it takes more than one sentence, you don't know your theme.
3. **No Clear Internal Genre**: Every memorable story has an Internal Genre driving the B Story.
4. **Complications Don't Escalate**: Each section must be MORE dire than the last.
5. **Crisis Has Obvious Answer**: If one choice is clearly better, it's not a real crisis.
6. **Missing Obligatory Scenes**: Check your genre requirements before finalizing.
