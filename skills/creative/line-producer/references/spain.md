## 1. Union & Labor Framework

### Labor Law (ET — Estatuto de los Trabajadores)
- Spain operates under strict EU labor law; no at-will employment
- All crew on productions >15 days effectively require labor contracts (contrato de obra)
- Maximum 10-hour shooting day (including travel); 12-hour calls are illegal without pre-agreed overtime protocols
- Mandatory 12-hour rest between calls; 36-hour continuous rest per week
- **Impact:** Spain shoots fewer pages per day than Mexico — 3–5 pages/day is realistic for drama

### AISGE (Artistas, Intérpretes o Ejecutantes)
- Collective management for performers; residual/secondary use fees
- Work-in-medium fees apply on distribution; budget separately from production fees

### Technicians — Collective Agreement (Convenio Colectivo)
- Active convenio for cinema technicians; applies to productions >certain budget thresholds
- Rates in convenio are minimums; above-convenio is negotiated
- Important: convenio applies to resident-in-Spain technicians; foreign crew on short visits may have different arrangements

## 3. Social Security & Fringes

Total loaded cost = gross wage × 1.30–1.33

| Component | Employer Rate |
|-----------|--------------|
| Seguridad Social (SS) | ~23.6% |
| Unemployment (FOGASA) | ~0.2% |
| Training levy | ~0.6% |
| Professional contingency (Acc. de Trabajo) | ~varies by category |
| Vacation accrual (30 days/year) | ~8.2% |
| **Total fringe** | **~30–33%** |

**Key:** Spain vacation is 30 working days/year (not calendar). Accrual on short-term contracts is included in the fringe calculation above.

**Freelance (autónomo):** Technicians working as autónomos pay their own SS (~31%); budget 5–10% premium over salaried rate as compensation. Verify: AEAT has been cracking down on false autónomos.

## 4. National Tax Incentives

### Deducción Fiscal por Producción Audiovisual (Art. 36.1 LIS)
- 30% tax deduction on first €1M of qualifying Spanish spend
- 25% tax deduction on spend above €1M
- Minimum Spanish spend: €1M
- Spanish production (Spain-registered entity required)
- Cap: deduction cannot exceed 50% of tax liability unless filed as a producer credit

### International Co-Production Deduction (Art. 36.2 LIS)
- 25% tax deduction for foreign productions spending in Spain
- Minimum Spanish spend: €1M
- Requirements: Spanish co-producer on contract, cultural test (must pass)
- **Game changer numbers:** A production spending €5M in Spain gets €1.25M back — very competitive vs. other EU territories

### Canary Islands Special Tax Zone (ZEC)
- 45–50% tax deduction (vs. 30% mainland) — highest in EU
- Available through Gran Canaria Film Commission
- Requires: company registered in Canarias, minimum spend in islands
- Best suited for productions that can genuinely base significant activity in Canarias

## 5. Regional Incentives

| Region | Program | Benefit |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Canary Islands | ZEC + cash rebate | 40–50% effective return on qualifying spend |
| País Vasco | Basque incentive | Additional ~10% on top of national deduction; Bilbao/San Sebastián logistics strong |
| Andalucía | FilmAndalucía | Cash rebate ~15% on qualifying local spend |
| Cataluña | Catalunya Film Commission | Soft incentives (location access, permits), no direct cash |
| Madrid | Madrid Film Office | Permit facilitation; no direct rebate |

**Canary Islands flag:** The Canarias incentive is real and significant but requires genuine production activity on the islands, not just financial routing. Experienced fixer strongly recommended.

## 7. Common Budget Mistakes in Spain

1. **Under-budgeting labor compliance** — 10-hour maximum shooting day feels restrictive coming from Mexico. Exceeding it triggers costly overtime and crew can refuse. Wrap day earlier, adjust schedule.

2. **Ignoring dietas** — Meal and travel allowances for out-of-city locations add €40–80/person/day. On a crew of 80 people for 3 weeks out of Madrid, that's €100k+ unbudgeted.

3. **Missing ZEC documentation requirements** — The Canary Islands incentive requires specific spend documentation from Day 1. Productions that discover this at delivery lose the incentive.

4. **False autónomos** — Using freelancers as autónomos to avoid SS is increasingly flagged by AEAT. Build proper SS into budgets.

5. **AISGE residuals not budgeted** — On-demand/streaming distributions trigger AISGE fees. Budget in distribution model.

6. **Cultural test failure** — International productions applying for the 36.2 deduction sometimes fail the cultural content test. Consult a Spanish cultural affairs consultant before committing to the incentive in the financing model.

7. **Underestimating post-production costs in Spain** — Good VFX and post facilities exist but at EU prices. Don't benchmark against MX or Eastern Europe costs.
