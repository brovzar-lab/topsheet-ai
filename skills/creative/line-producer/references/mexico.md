## 1. Union Landscape

### STPC (Sindicato de Trabajadores de la Producción Cinematográfica)
- Covers most theatrical feature film productions and premium TV
- Mandatory for productions receiving IMCINE support or EFICINE
- Wage tables negotiated periodically; current tables from 2023 STPC negotiations
- Key requirements: daily wage minimums, mandatory rest between calls (10h), mandatory Sunday premium (25%), overtime after 8h (25% first 2h, 50% beyond)

### STIC (Sindicato de Trabajadores de la Industria Cinematográfica)
- Co-exists with STPC; jurisdiction determined by company registration and production type
- Some departments (labs, post, distribution) historically STIC territory
- Strategic choice: STPC vs STIC affects which crew is available and at what minimums

### Non-Union
- Legal for productions that don't trigger union obligations
- Lower fringes (~25–30% vs ~35–42%) but limited access to experienced union crew
- Common for commercials, branded content, low-budget features under $5M MXN

### ANDA (Asociación Nacional de Actores)
- Covers principal cast
- Minimum daily rates: ~$4,500–8,000 MXN/day for day players (2024 est.)
- Weekly rates negotiated; billing extras (featured extras), stunt doubles have own categories
- Always budget ANDA fringe (~13%) on top of gross cast fees

## 3. Social Costs & Fringes

### STPC Union Production
Total loaded cost = gross wage × 1.38–1.42

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| IMSS (Seguro Social) | ~22–25% |
| INFONAVIT | 5% |
| SAR (Retirement) | 2% |
| Vacation pay accrual | ~4–5% |
| Sunday premium accrual | ~2–3% |
| Holiday premium provision | ~1% |
| **Total fringe** | **~35–42%** |

### Non-Union
Total loaded cost ≈ gross wage × 1.25–1.30

- IMSS still mandatory (illegal to avoid)
- No union vacation/premium provisions
- Lower compliance overhead but less experienced crew pool

### On-Camera Cast (ANDA)
On top of gross talent fee:
- ANDA fringe: ~13%
- IMSS (if not registered as independent): ~22–25%
- Common practice: talent on honorarios (independent contractor) to reduce burden — verify with accountant

## 4. Tax Incentives

### EFICINE 189 (National Screen Trust)
- 10% tax credit on qualifying MX production spend
- Requires IMCINE certification as Mexican production
- Minimum budget thresholds apply; changes annually
- **Key trap:** Documentation must be audit-ready from Day 1. Don't assume you can reconstruct it post-wrap.
- Timeline: credit applied in fiscal year of investment, not delivery — coordinate with investor's tax position

### EFICINE 226 (Padrón)
- For cinema exhibition investments
- Different investor profile — less common for production financing

### State Incentives
| State | Program | Typical Benefit |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| CDMX | Estímulos CDMX | Cash rebate on qualifying local spend, ~5–10% |
| Jalisco | Jalisco en Paz / FECJ | Location discounts + cash on local crew/vendor spend |
| Baja California | Baja Incentive | Sales tax exemption on production spend |
| Yucatán | Yucatán Film | Up to 10% cash incentive on in-state spend |
| Nuevo León | Various | Negotiated case-by-case |

**Flag:** State programs are inconsistent year-to-year. Verify current status before including in a financing plan.

## 5. Permit Requirements

| Requirement | Lead Time | Who Issues |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| IMCINE classification (Mexican film) | 4–6 weeks | IMCINE |
| CDMX filming permit (public spaces) | 2–4 weeks | Secretaría de Cultura CDMX / LOCATEL |
| SAT production registration | Before payroll | SAT |
| Weapons (firearms on set) | 4–8 weeks | SEDENA — starts early, never sufficient |
| Drone operations | 2–3 weeks | AFAC (Agencia Federal de Aviación Civil) |
| Night shoot permit (CDMX) | Add 1–2 weeks to standard | Delegación + Secretaría |
| Animal work | 2–4 weeks | SEMARNAT / PROFEPA |

**SEDENA flag:** Even prop/deactivated firearms need SEDENA permits. Budget 4–8 weeks minimum. This is a common schedule killer.

## 6. Common Budget Mistakes in Mexico

1. **Under-budgeting fringes** — Producers routinely use 25% when union payroll requires 38–42%. This creates a $2–4M MXN gap on a $30M production.

2. **Missing STPC union minimums** — Budgeting at "what we paid last time" without checking current STPC tables.

3. **Location fees in CDMX** — High-demand locations (Roma Norte, Polanco, Centro Histórico) have tripled in price since 2020. Always verify current market.

4. **Forgetting SAT compliance costs** — Accountant fees, CFDI issuance, electronic invoicing setup are real line items.

5. **No SEDENA line item** — Weapons handling coordinator + SEDENA liaison is often zero-budgeted and surprises productions mid-prep.

6. **Fuel/transportation at 2019 prices** — Diesel, rental vehicles, and driver rates have risen sharply.

7. **Art department underestimates for period/set build** — Mexican art departments are skilled and affordable vs. US/EU, but non-Mexico-City locations add significant transport/logistics cost.

8. **No cash position buffer** — EFICINE reimbursement timing is often 6–12 months after investment. Budget a credit line for the gap.
