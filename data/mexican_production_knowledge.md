# Mexican Film Production — Budgeting Reference

This file contains practical cost data and labor rules for budgeting Mexican feature films. Use it to supplement the Master Price Index when estimating costs.

## Labor & Union Rules

### ANDA (Actors)
- Base rates are confidential; use MPI values as benchmarks
- Mandatory fringes on top of base rate: **13%** (3% culture bonus + 3% productivity bonus + aguinaldo + prima vacacional)
- Union dues (deducted from actor pay, not production cost): 15%
- ISR withholding: 10% retained by union
- No streaming residuals exist in Mexico — pay is per work day only
- Categories: Estelar (Lead), 1a Parte (Co-Lead), 2a Parte (Supporting), Bit (Day Player), Extra

### STPC / ANTEC (Crew)
- Standard shooting week: **67 hours** (Mon–Fri 12hrs + Sat 7hrs)
- ANTEC minimum weekly rates (floors — market rates are 2–5× higher):
  - DP: $13,000 MXN/week
  - 1st AD: $7,450 MXN/week
  - Camera Operator: $7,350 MXN/week
  - Gaffer: $5,750 MXN/week
  - Key Grip: $5,750 MXN/week
  - Script Supervisor: $6,650 MXN/week
  - Production Manager: $6,250 MXN/week
- Only ~35% of Mexico's ~240 annual productions use unionized labor

### Employer Fringes (All Workers)
- IMSS + INFONAVIT + SAR: **30–40% of gross wages**
- INFONAVIT housing: 5% of base
- 2026 UMA: $113.14 MXN/day
- 2026 general minimum wage: $315.04 MXN/day

### Overtime (Ley Federal del Trabajo)
- First 9 OT hours/week: **double time (200%)**
- Beyond 9 OT hours/week: **triple time (300%)**
- Budget 5–8% of BTL labor for OT allowance depending on genre/complexity

## Permits (CDMX)

- Standard vehicular street closure: **$5,729 MXN/day**
- Bike lanes / pedestrian areas: **$1,149 MXN/day**
- Central/high-impact zones: **+50% surcharge**
- Urgent 24hr permits: **double standard rate**
- Private locations: negotiated directly, no set rates

## Production Schedules by Tier

| Phase | Low (MXN 2–10M) | Mid (MXN 10–30M) | Premium (MXN 30M+) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-production | 4–8 weeks | 8–12 weeks | 12–20 weeks |
| Principal photography | 15–25 days | 25–35 days | 30–50 days |
| Post-production | 8–16 weeks | 16–24 weeks | 24–40 weeks |

6-day shoot week is standard. Budget prep/wrap weeks for crew at reduced rates (typically 60–75% of shoot rate).

## Standard Fees & Contingencies

- **Contingency**: 10% of total (5% low-risk, up to 15% action/remote)
- **Production company overhead**: 10–15% of BTL
- **Producer fees**: flat rate or 5–10% of total budget
- **Completion bond** (if required): 3–6% of total budget

## Exchange Rate

- Current: ~17.2 MXN/USD (Feb 2026)
- Budget at a conservative rate (17.5–18.0) to hedge depreciation risk
- For international co-productions, lock rates early
